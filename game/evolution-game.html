Extinction Event

The population has died out.

This is what happens when we eliminate deviation.

Quetelet believed that deviation from the average should be culled, that the "average man" was the ideal. But evolution teaches us that diversity—deviation from the mean—is what allows populations to survive catastrophic change.

Without variation, a single environmental shift can destroy everything.
